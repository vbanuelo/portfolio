The Birth of an Icon

Created by animator Max Fleischer in 1930, Betty Boop became the epitome of the Jazz Age spirit. With her flapper style and infectious personality, she captured hearts worldwide. Her original drawing was that of a dog.

Cultural Impact

Betty Boop represented the liberated woman of the 1920s and 30s. Her bold personality and signature catchphrase made her a cultural phenomenon that influenced fashion and popular culture.

Artistic Legacy

The character's distinctive art style revolutionized animation. Betty's expressive animations set new standards for character design and storytelling in the medium.

Timeless Appeal

Nearly a century later, Betty Boop remains an enduring symbol of glamour and fun. Her image continues to appear in contemporary media, proving that great design stands the test of time.
